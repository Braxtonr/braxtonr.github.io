In 2016, LeBron James delivered on his promise to bring the first championship to his hometown's team, the Cleveland Cavaliers. The promise came after he decided to leave his hometown team in 2010 to join the Miami Heat where he won his first and second championship. Throughout the NBA finals, LeBron averaged nearly a triple double while leading both teams in each statistical category. He also blocked the shot of Andre Igudaoula that helped seal the pivotal game 7. The block is argued as the best block in NBA history and dubbed "The Block". LeBron's team came back from a 3-1 deficit and became the only team in NBA history to do so and win the championship. The table below shows LeBron's stats for the 7 game series.

1st in Points 29.7
1st in Rebounds 11.3
1st in Assists 8.9
1st in Steals 2.6
1st in Blocks 2.3
