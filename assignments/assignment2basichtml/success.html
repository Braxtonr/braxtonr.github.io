In 2016, LeBron James delivered on his promise to bring the first championship to his hometown's basketball team, the Cleveland Cavaliers. The promise came after he decided to leave the Cavaliers in 2010 to join the Miami Heat where he won his first and second championship. Throughout the 2016 NBA finals, LeBron averaged nearly a triple double while leading both teams in each statistical category. He also blocked the shot of Andre Igudaoula that helped seal the victory. The play is argued as the best block in NBA history and famously named "The Block".
